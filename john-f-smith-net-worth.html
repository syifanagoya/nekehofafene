John F. Smith Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

John was born in the heart of Leicestershire in 1950 and demonstrated an affinity for music from a young age. He honed his skills at the Leicestershire County School of Music and later at the prestigious Royal Academy of Music in London.

It was here that John not only bagged the Sidney Langston Prize for Brass playing but also secured a Licensiate diploma (LRAM), setting the stage for a distinguished musical career.

Career Milestones

In 1974, John joined the English National Opera (ENO) as Principal Tuba, a role he relished for nearly two decades.

His passion for music was equally matched by his commitment to education, as he pursued a B.Sc. in politics and sociology from the Open University, further enriching his understanding of the socio-political landscape of the music industry.

Transition to Union Leadership
